The Earl Who Loved His Lunch

2026-07-14 · How Things Came to Be · a true story · 3-minute read

Imagine a man in England, back in the 1700s. He was a very busy nobleman, John Montagu, the 4th Earl of Sandwich. He had important jobs to do for the country, working with the navy and in politics. He also loved art and other interests.

Because he was always so busy, he often had to eat while he was working. Sometimes, he was at his desk, trying to get things done. Other times, he might have been enjoying a game of cards.

When he needed to eat, he didn't want to stop his work or his games for too long. He also didn't want to get his hands messy. So, he asked his helpers to bring him his food in a very clever way. He would have roast beef, which is a type of cooked meat, placed between two pieces of bread. This way, he could hold it easily and eat it without needing a fork. It was also a quick meal.

This simple, practical way of eating became very popular. People saw what he was doing and started to copy him. Soon, this dish with meat between bread was called a 'sandwich' after the Earl.

At first, people thought of it as a food for busy men eating late at night. But over time, the idea spread. The sandwich became a convenient food for everyone.

As more people learned about this tasty meal, it traveled from London to other parts of England. By the 1800s, with more people working in factories and needing quick meals, sandwiches became even more important. They were easy to carry and eat on the go.

Today, the sandwich is loved all over the world. It’s a common lunch for people going to work or school. From its simple beginnings with a busy Earl, the sandwich has become a favorite meal for millions.
